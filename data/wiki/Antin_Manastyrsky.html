Antin Manastyrsky

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search
Self-portrait of Antin Manastyrsky

Antin Ivanovych Manastyrsky (Ukrainian: Антін Іванович Манастирський; November 2, 1878 – May 15, 1969) was an Ukrainian folk artist, painter, and graphic artist. He is the father of artist Vitold Manastyrsky.

Manastyrsky was born in the village of Zavaliv, now Pidhaitsi Raion of the Ternopil region, in the family of a postal official. He graduated from the Lviv Art-Industrial School and later the Krakow Academy of Fine Arts.

In 1900, the Society for the Advancement of the Ruthenian Work, which Manastyrsky was a member of, staged an exhibition of his first paintings. Since that time, he began his artistic career. He paints a whole gallery of high-spiritual works - landscapes, contemporary reality, and satires. Manastyrsky lived and worked in Lviv.

Manastyrsky died May 15, 1969, and was buried in a family tomb at the Lychakiv cemetery.
