Ravenel B. Curry III is an American businessman and philanthropist.[1][2][3]

Biography[edit]

Early life[edit]

He graduated from Furman University in 1963.[2]

Career[edit]

He is the President of Eagle Capital Management, an investment management firm headquartered in New York City which he co-Founded with Beth Curry.[4][1][3]

Philanthropy[edit]

He sits on the Board of Trustees of the American Enterprise Institute, the Manhattan Institute for Policy Research, The Duke Endowment, the New York Hall of Science, the New-York Historical Society, the Genetics Endowment of South Carolina, the , and his alma mater, Furman University.[1][3][5][6][7][8] In 2004, he donated US$1 million to support the Chinese Studies program at Furman University.[2] He and Beth Curry founded the Ravenel and Elizabeth Curry Foundation, which made major contributions to Rockefeller University, Weill Cornell Medical College and Queens University of Charlotte, N.C.[4]

Personal life[edit]

He married Elizabeth Rivers Curry (née Mary Elizabeth Rivers) (1941-2015) in 1963.[2][4]
