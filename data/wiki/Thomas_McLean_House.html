Thomas McLean House is a historic home located at Battenville in Washington County, New York. It was built between about 1795 and 1867 and consists of a five-bay, 2-story main block with two ​1 1⁄2-story wings. Also on the property are two timber-frame barns, a 1-story commercial building, shed, and remains of a stone foundation. It is located across from the Stoops Hotel.[2]

It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2007.[1]
