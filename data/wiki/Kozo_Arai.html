Kozo Arai (荒井 公三, Arai Kōzō, born October 24, 1950) is a former Japanese footballer. He played for Japan national team.

Club career[edit]

Arai was born in Etajima on October 24, 1950. After graduating from high school, he joined Furukawa Electric in 1969. In 1976, the club won Japan Soccer League and Emperor's Cup. The club also won 1977 JSL Cup. He retired in 1978. He played 124 games and scored 17 goals in the league. He was elected Best Eleven 4 times (1971, 1973, 1974 and 1976).

National team career[edit]

In December 1970, Arai was elected Japan national team for 1970 Asian Games. At this competition, on December 10, he debuted against Malaysia. He also played at 1974 Asian Games, 1972 Summer Olympics qualification, 1974 World Cup qualification and 1976 Summer Olympics qualification. He played 47 games and scored 4 goals for Japan until 1977.[1]
